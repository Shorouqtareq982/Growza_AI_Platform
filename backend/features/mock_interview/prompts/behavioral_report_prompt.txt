INTERPRETATION GUIDE:

Big Five Personality (0–1 scale):
- Below 0.4 = low, 0.4–0.6 = average, above 0.6 = high
- Extraversion: social energy and assertiveness
- Agreeableness: cooperativeness and warmth
- Conscientiousness: organization and reliability
- Neuroticism: emotional reactivity (lower is more stable)
- Openness: creativity and curiosity

Visual Metrics:
- eye_contact_percentage: 0–100%. Higher is better. Above 80% is strong.
- head_stability_score: 0–1. Higher means more stable. Above 0.85 is good.
- smile_frequency: 0–1. Professional range is 0.2–0.4. Too high or too low can signal discomfort.
- face_visibility: 0–1. 1.0 means fully visible throughout.
- head_movement_variance: lower means more controlled. Above 10 may signal nervousness.

Audio Metrics:
- speaking_rate_wpm: Ideal interview range is 120–160 wpm. Below 110 is slow, above 170 is rushed.
- volume_consistency: 0 = perfectly consistent. Negative values mean the candidate got quieter over time.
- voice_variation_score: 0–1. Higher means more expressive. 0.0 = fully monotone.
- filler_words_per_minute: Lower is better. 0 = excellent. Above 3 is distracting.
- avg_pause_duration: in seconds. Long pauses may signal hesitation.
- long_pause_count: number of pauses above threshold. Higher may indicate uncertainty.
- speech_clarity: 0–1. 1.0 = perfectly clear articulation.

Text Metrics:
- word_count: total words spoken. Below 50 is very brief and may indicate underdeveloped answers.
- avg_sentence_length: 10–15 words is a natural conversational range.
- lexical_diversity: 0–1. Higher means richer vocabulary. Above 0.7 is strong.
- confidence_score: 0–1. Measures confident language patterns. 0.0 = very low.
- filler_word_rate: per 100 words. Below 1 is excellent. Above 3 is noticeable.
- complexity_score: 0–1. Very low (below 0.2) means simple language. Context-dependent.
- structure_score: 0–1. Higher means better organized responses.
- clarity_score: 0–1. Higher means clearer communication.
